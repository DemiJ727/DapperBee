What is Stress?

Stress can range from the short to long term. In both cases there are ways that people can work to reduce stress levels. While not all stress is bad in moderation, it is still important for all people to reduce stress levels when they can. The reason behind this is because there are many negative side effects and consequences due to long term stress exposure.

According to the American Psychology Association, there are several different forms of stress. The following definitions come from Healthline.

The 3 Types of Stress

Acute Stress: This is the most common type of stress. It is the body's immediate reaction to a situation that can be described as activating your "fight, flight, or freeze" response.

Episodic Acute Stress: When acute stress becomes reccuring but is not necessarily constant it is considered to be episodic acute stress.

Chronic Stress: This is when acute stress is not resolved, is long lasting, or events continue to occur to the point where the stress feels constant.

What does it do to you?


While there are different types are stress, if stress becomes chronic over time it can cause or contribute to many other health conditions. According to Healthline stress in genderal can have many effects on the body, such as:

  • Headaches and Tense Muscles
  • Increased Depression and Insonmnia
  • Rapid Breathing
  • Weakened Immune System
  • Pounding Heart and risk of Heart Attack
  • High Blood Sugar and Pressure
  • Reproductive System Issues in both men and women

Many people are able to live happily and manage stress effectively. However, other people can suffer from versions of anxiety, depression, etc. which can heavily affect their lives in relationships and doing simple things like even going to a store.

In cases like these, it is important that the individual learns what stress management techniques work for them. Individuals that believe they may be suffering from one of the above disorders should also speak with a medical professional, such as a healthcare provider, psychologist or psychiatrist.
